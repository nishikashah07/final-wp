Top Places to Visit in Nagaland

Kohima

The capital city, known for the WWII Cemetery, and breathtaking views of the surrounding hills.

Dimapur

The largest city in Nagaland, known for its historical ruins and vibrant markets.

Mokokchung

Famous for its vibrant Ao Naga culture, traditional festivals, and scenic landscapes.

Mon

Known for the Konyak Naga tribe, unique cultural heritage, and the annual Aoleang festival.

Wokha

Popular for its scenic hills, rivers, and the colorful Tokhu Emong festival.

Dzukou Valley

A mesmerizing valley known for its lush landscapes, flower-covered slopes, and trekking trails.

Pfutsero

The coldest inhabited place in Nagaland, famous for its apple farms and tranquil surroundings.

Khonoma

India's first green village, known for its conservation efforts and lush terraced fields.

Mount Saramati

The highest peak in Nagaland, popular among trekkers and adventure enthusiasts.

Japfu Peak

Famous for its scenic beauty, with a breathtaking view of the surrounding valleys.

Travel and Stay Options

Travel: Nagaland is connected by road, with the main airport located in Dimapur. Buses, taxis, and shared jeeps are commonly used for local travel.

Stay: Options include guesthouses, homestays, and eco-friendly resorts. Popular areas to stay include Kohima and Dimapur.

Popular Delicacies: Traditional Naga dishes like Smoked Pork, Bamboo Shoot Curry, and local delicacies such as Axone and Zutho (rice beer) are must-tries.
