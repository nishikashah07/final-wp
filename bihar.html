Top Places to Visit in Bihar

Bodh Gaya

The site where Lord Buddha attained enlightenment under the Bodhi Tree. The Mahabodhi Temple is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Nalanda

Home to the ancient Nalanda University, a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its historical and academic significance.

Rajgir

A historic town known for its hot springs, ancient sites, and scenic hills. It is significant in both Buddhism and Jainism.

Patna

The capital city with rich history, featuring landmarks like the Golghar, Patna Museum, and Gandhi Maidan.

Vaishali

An ancient city important to both Buddhists and Jains, known for its archaeological ruins and Ashokan Pillar.

Vikramshila

The remains of an ancient university, known for being a center of Buddhist learning in ancient India.

Pawapuri

A sacred site for Jains, where Lord Mahavira attained Nirvana. The Jal Mandir is a popular pilgrimage spot.

Bhagalpur

Known as the "Silk City," Bhagalpur is famous for its production of Tussar silk and its rich culture.

Kesaria Stupa

The largest stupa in India, believed to have been built by Emperor Ashoka. It holds immense historical importance.

Barabar Caves

The oldest surviving rock-cut caves in India, dating back to the Mauryan period, known for their historical and archaeological significance.

Travel and Stay Options

Travel: Bihar is well connected by rail, road, and air. The major airport is in Patna, and the state has a network of trains and buses for intercity travel.

Stay: Accommodation options range from budget lodges to luxury hotels, particularly in cities like Patna, Bodh Gaya, and Rajgir.
