Top Places to Visit in Manipur

Imphal

The capital city, known for the Kangla Fort, beautiful landscapes, and rich cultural heritage.

Loktak Lake

India's largest freshwater lake with unique phumdis (floating islands) and stunning scenery.

Moreh

A bustling border town, popular for shopping and experiencing the cultural mix of India and Myanmar.

Keibul Lamjao National Park

The only floating national park in the world, home to the endangered Sangai deer.

Bishnupur

Known for its ancient temples, especially the 15th-century Vishnu temple made of bricks.

Ukhrul

A serene hill town, famous for the rare Shirui Lily and its beautiful landscapes.

Tharon Caves

A fascinating cave system with ancient rock carvings, ideal for adventure seekers.

Andro

A traditional village known for its pottery, cultural museum, and indigenous art forms.

Singda Dam

A scenic dam near Imphal, offering panoramic views and a peaceful retreat.

Kakching

Known for its beautiful gardens, temples, and annual festivals showcasing Manipuri culture.

Travel and Stay Options

Travel: Imphal Airport connects Manipur with major Indian cities. Local travel options include taxis and buses for convenient exploration.

Stay: Accommodation ranges from budget guesthouses in Imphal to eco-lodges around Loktak Lake for nature lovers.

Popular Delicacies: Try local dishes like Eromba (a tangy fish dish), Chamthong (vegetable stew), and Ngari (fermented fish).
