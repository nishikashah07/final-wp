Top Places to Visit in Delhi

Red Fort

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this historic fort is an iconic symbol of India's rich history and architecture.

Qutub Minar

The tallest brick minaret in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, showcasing intricate Indo-Islamic architecture.

India Gate

A war memorial dedicated to Indian soldiers who lost their lives in World War I, set amidst beautiful gardens.

Lotus Temple

Known for its unique lotus-like shape, this Bahá'í House of Worship is a place of peace and spirituality.

Humayun's Tomb

A magnificent example of Mughal architecture and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, considered a precursor to the Taj Mahal.

Jama Masjid

One of the largest mosques in India, built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan, offering stunning views of Old Delhi.

Akshardham Temple

A modern architectural marvel that showcases India's rich cultural heritage, with intricate carvings and exhibitions.

Chandni Chowk

A bustling market in Old Delhi, known for its narrow lanes, historic sites, and diverse street food offerings.

Rashtrapati Bhavan

The official residence of the President of India, known for its majestic architecture and the Mughal Gardens.

Lodhi Gardens

A serene historical garden with tombs from the Lodi and Sayyid dynasties, perfect for picnics and nature walks.

Travel and Stay Options

Travel: Delhi is well connected by air, rail, and road. Indira Gandhi International Airport serves as a major hub, and the metro system offers efficient city travel.

Stay: Accommodation ranges from luxury hotels in Connaught Place to budget hostels and guesthouses in Paharganj.

Popular Delicacies: Don’t miss trying local dishes like Chole Bhature, Parathas from Chandni Chowk, and Delhi’s famous street food such as Golgappas and Aloo Tikki.
