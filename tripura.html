Top Places to Visit in Tripura

Neermahal

The majestic water palace situated in the middle of Rudrasagar Lake, showcasing a blend of Hindu and Mughal architecture.

Unakoti

A historic site with rock carvings and murals dating back to the 7th-9th centuries, known as a hidden treasure of Tripura.

Tripura Sundari Temple

One of the 51 Shakti Peethas, famous for its spiritual significance and architecture.

Sipahijala Wildlife Sanctuary

A sanctuary rich in flora and fauna, ideal for birdwatching and home to clouded leopards.

Jatrapur Lake

A serene lake known for its natural beauty and as a popular spot for bird lovers.

Ujjayanta Palace

An iconic palace turned museum in Agartala, showcasing Tripura's rich cultural heritage.

Dumboor Lake

A scenic spot with small islands and a popular destination for boat rides and picnics.

Kamalasagar Lake

A picturesque lake near the Bangladesh border, with a historic Kali temple adjacent to it.

Pilak Archaeological Site

An ancient site known for its terracotta sculptures and stone carvings, revealing Tripura's historic artistry.

Boxanagar Buddhist Site

A significant archaeological site with remains of Buddhist stupas and monasteries, showcasing the state's historical connection to Buddhism.

Travel and Stay Options

Travel: The state capital, Agartala, is well connected by road and air. Local transportation includes buses and taxis for getting around.

Stay: Options range from luxury hotels in Agartala to comfortable guesthouses in the towns and countryside.

Popular Delicacies: Don't miss out on dishes like Mui Borok (traditional fish stew), Bamboo Shoot Pickles, and local rice beer.
